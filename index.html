Spanish Architecture – Gaudí & More

Spanish architecture is a rich blend of cultures and eras. It began with Roman and Visigoth foundations, then flourished under the Moors, who introduced intricate arches, courtyards, and decorative patterns, as seen in the Alhambra. After the Reconquista, Gothic cathedrals like the Barcelona Cathedral combined European styles with local traditions, while the Renaissance and Baroque added grandeur and ornamentation. In the 19th–20th centuries, Modernisme, led by Antoni Gaudí, brought organic shapes and imaginative designs, exemplified by the Sagrada Familia. Across Spain, architecture reflects a layering of history, culture, and regional identity, from medieval stone churches to vibrant, modernist masterpieces.

Here's a quick summary of the main topics:

Sagrada Familia – Barcelona
Type: Basilica / Modernist Architecture
Architect: Antoni Gaudí
Construction: 1882 – ongoing (expected ~2030)
Highlights: Unique mix of Gothic and Modernisme; 18 spires; tree-like interior columns; colorful stained glass; UNESCO World Heritage Site
Location: Carrer de Mallorca, Barcelona
Alhambra – Granada
Type: Palace & Fortress / Islamic (Moorish) Architecture
Construction: 13th–14th centuries, Nasrid dynasty
Highlights: Nasrid Palaces with intricate stucco and arabesques; Court of the Lions; Generalife gardens; Alcazaba fortress with city views; UNESCO World Heritage Site
Location: Granada, Andalusia
Barcelona Cathedral – Gothic Quarter, Barcelona
Type: Cathedral / Gothic Architecture
Construction: 1298–1448
Highlights: Catalan Gothic style; pointed arches and ribbed vaults; cloister with 13 geese and Saint Eulalia’s crypt; central spire ~70 meters; rooftop views over the Gothic Quarter
Location: Pla de la Seu, Barcelona
Casa Batlló – Passeig de Gràcia, Barcelona
Type: Modernist Architecture / Cultural Landmark
Architect: Antoni Gaudí
Reconstruction: 1904–1906
Highlights: Colorful mosaic façade made with trencadís; flowing, organic shapes typical of Catalan Modernism; balconies resembling masks; roof designed like a dragon’s back symbolizing the legend of Saint George; innovative use of light wells and ventilation
Location: Passeig de Gràcia 43, Barcelona
